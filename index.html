About

About

I am a DPhil student in the Computational Neuroscience Lab at the University of Oxford, supervised by Prof. Tim Behrens and supported by the Boehringer Ingelheim Fonds and the German Scholarship Foundation.

My research aims to understand how neural systems encode structured knowledge that supports planning, abstraction, and generalisation. I combine computational modelling with analyses of human fMRI and invasive electrophysiology, together with complementary rodent data, to test mechanistic hypotheses about higher cognition.

Before my PhD, I completed a BSc in Psychology (Heidelberg) and MSc degrees in Neuro-Cognitive Psychology (LMU Munich) and Neuroscience (Oxford).

Research

Current Project

Neural representations of future states and schemas
DPhil in Computational Neuroscience, University of Oxford
2022 – present

I study how the brain represents abstract structure to support flexible behaviour. My work combines multivariate analyses of fMRI, human iEEG (LFP and spikes), and rodent recordings with computational models inspired by reinforcement learning and representation learning.
