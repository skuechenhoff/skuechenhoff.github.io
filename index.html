Research

Neural coding for planning and schema generalisation
DPhil in Computational Neuroscience · University of Oxford
2022 – present
I investigate how neural populations encode structured knowledge that supports planning and generalisation. I use computational models and multivariate analyses to link human fMRI and invasive iEEG (LFP and spikes) with rodent recordings, testing mechanistic hypotheses that go beyond correlational descriptions.
Research interests
I am interested in:
  • Algorithmic accounts of higher cognition (planning, abstraction, model-based behaviour)
  • Neural representations that support generalisation across tasks and contexts
  • Bridging human and rodent data to build mechanistic, cross-species models
  • Interfaces between systems neuroscience, reinforcement learning, and representation learning
