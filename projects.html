TReND-CaMinA — Teaching & Student Projects (2025 / 2026)

Lecturer & tutor — MRI lecture, Python tutorials, student project supervision

At TReND-CaMinA I led an introductory lecture on how MRI can be used for computational neuroscience and ran hands-on Python tutorials tailored to students with mixed backgrounds. I supervised a student group project analysing an Allen Institute calcium-imaging dataset — designing a reproducible analysis pipeline, mentoring the group through data preprocessing and visualization, and guiding interpretation of layer-specific activity patterns in visual cortex.

In this role I organised workshop materials to be clear and reusable, split the project into manageable milestones, and facilitated presentations so students could communicate methods and results. The emphasis was on enabling accessible, reproducible analyses and helping participants convert data insights into well-formed short talks and project reports.
